Round Light Object "Japanese Paper"

The round light object "Japanese paper" with a diameter of 90 cm and a copper-colored frame is covered with an original Japanese white Shoji paper and is illuminated from inside (multicolored).

Shoji-papers are traditionally used in Japan for covering sliding doors and, due to their translucency, are also very well suited for luminaries' construction. This shoji paper is natural white and has a very nice hemp fiber-water drop structure.

The frame has been treated with several layers of sheet metal (gilded metal) and connected to the shoji paper on the front by a narrow copper rim.

Inside, the luminary is covered with over 500 individual RGB LEDs and illuminated evenly, so that the individual drops of water and hemp fibers show up very well.

The light can be controlled and dimmed via a remote control. It can be selected over 16 different shades by remote control.

Round Light Object "Water"

The round light object "Water" with a diameter of 90 cm and a copper-colored, oxidized frame is lit from the inside (white).

The motif is a water close-up with various blue and single yellow tones, which is applied as a foil on a round acrylic glass plate.

On the round frame several layers of copper (striking metal) are applied ("gold plated").

The copper was oxidized in several steps and treated with faceted lacquer, so that a very beautiful turquoise tone was created on the copper, which combines with the image motif.

Inside, the luminary is covered with white LED modules and evenly illuminated, which makes the individual colors of the water stand out very well, producing different images depending on the lighting intensity.

The light can be controlled and dimmed via a remote control.

  • Diameter: 90 cm, height: 7 cm

Round Light Object "Skycrystal NYC"

The round light object "Skycrystal NYC" with a diameter of 88 cm and a hand painted frame is lit from the inside (white).

The motif is a close-up of a thick, textured glass front that was shot high above the rooftops of New York City.

On the round frame several layers of white, iron and rust colors are applied, which melt into each other and are reflected in the colors of the picture.

The motif is applied to a sheet of acrylic glass by means of a foil and illuminated by means of white LED modules. Due to the lighting, the glass structures and colors gain additional depth and shine.

The light can be controlled and dimmed via a remote control.
